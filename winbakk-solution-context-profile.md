# Solution Context Profile: *Winbakk*

## Overview

*Winbakk* is an automated Customer Success platform for B2C SMBs that transforms existing customer bases (name, WhatsApp, and purchase history) into *predictable incremental revenue* through intelligent relationship automations via **WhatsApp (primary)** and **email**, with setup in under **20 minutes** and minimal (or zero) human intervention.

The core proposition is to operate as *"Service as a Software"*: the business owner doesn't configure complex flows — Winbakk executes post-sale for them and delivers **real ROI**, measured in incremental revenue.

---

## Section 1: Problem Mapping

### Surface Problem

What the customer says:

* "My sales have dropped."
* "I have customers, but I can't get them to come back."
* "I have customers' WhatsApp numbers, but I don't have time to work on that."
* "I've tried CRM/loyalty programs and nobody uses them."
* "Paid marketing keeps getting more expensive."

### Real Problem

The problem isn't acquisition, it's *the absence of an executable post-sale system*.

The SMB:

* has data but doesn't turn it into action,
* doesn't know *who* to contact, *when*, or *with what message*,
* depends on human effort, memory, and improvisation,
* doesn't measure repurchase clearly.

No system → relationships become the exception, not a process.

### Adjacent Problems (domino effect)

* Unpredictable revenue and cash flow instability
* Excessive dependence on paid media and promotions
* Compressed margins (discounts to "make cash flow")
* Owner stuck in operations, always "putting out fires"
* Skepticism about new tools ("I've already wasted time and money")

### Root Cause

SMBs lack:

* *simple post-sale infrastructure*,
* *ready-made playbooks by objective*,
* *automation integrated with WhatsApp*,
* *clear ROI metric (incremental revenue)*.

Existing solutions are *too complex* (traditional CRM) or *too superficial* (manual messaging), and none take execution as the software's responsibility.

## Section 2: Value Chain Mapping

### Current State Costs

**Money**

* Between *20% and 40% of the customer base* becomes inactive over time.
* In recurring businesses (pharmacy, aesthetics, pet services), this easily represents *3% to 15% of monthly revenue lost*.
* Conservative example: SMB with R$80k/month → *R$2.4k to R$12k/month* not recovered.

**Time**

* Owner/manager spends *4–10 hours weekly* trying to plan campaigns, send messages, respond to customers, and track results — without a method.

**Opportunity Cost**

* Can't plan growth.
* Can't invest with confidence (inventory, team, equipment).
* Misses natural repurchase windows.

### Transformation Value

**Money**

* Conservative recovery of *3% to 10% monthly incremental revenue*, measured directly.
* Ex.: R$80k/month → *+R$2.4k to +R$8k/month*.

**Time**

* Recovers *3–8 hours weekly*, as execution becomes automatic.

**Opportunities**

* Post-sale becomes a continuous process.
* Customer communication stops being generic.
* Relationships start generating data, not just conversation.

### Downstream Value Unlocked

1. *Revenue predictability* → better planning and less defensive decisions.
2. *Reduced dependence on paid media* → improves LTV/CAC and margin.
3. *Learning from real data* → messages, offers, and cadences become increasingly efficient.

### Total Ecosystem Value

* **Direct transformation:** R$2.4k to R$8k/month
* **Downstream (efficiency + margin):** R$1k to R$4k/month

**Estimated total value:** *R$3.4k to R$12k/month per customer*

This strongly supports an accessible SaaS model (R$49–199/month) with *near-immediate payback*.

## Section 3: Solution Strategy

### The Method

1. **Minimal data input**

   * Name
   * WhatsApp
   * Purchases (date, value, items/service)

2. **Automatic base diagnosis**

   * Simple segmentation (e.g., RFM, time since last purchase, recurrence).

3. **Ready-made playbooks by objective**

   * Reactivation
   * Repurchase
   * Post-service check-in
   * Feedback
   * Key dates

4. **Automatic AI-generated messages**

   * Personalized by segment
   * Natural language (anti-spam)
   * Variations for testing

5. **Automated execution**

   * WhatsApp as primary channel
   * Email as complement
   * Minimal or zero approval required

6. **Clear result measurement**

   * Incremental revenue as the central metric.

7. **Continuous learning cycle**

   * What generates results becomes the standard.

### Why It Works

Because Winbakk *takes ownership of execution*, not just the tool.

The core insight is that SMBs:

* don't need to "configure flows",
* need an *automated service disguised as software*.

When you reduce setup, reduce human decision-making, and focus on WhatsApp + ROI, post-sale stops being a promise and becomes routine.

### What Makes It Defensible

* **Playbooks and benchmarks by vertical**, fed by real performance data.
* **Explicit measurement of incremental revenue**, not vanity metrics.
* **Radically plug-and-play experience** (20 minutes to go live).
* **"Service as a Software" positioning**, rare in CS/CRM.
* **Deep focus on WhatsApp**, where SMBs already operate.

> Competitors can copy features.
> What they can't easily copy is *data + method + frictionless execution*.

### Future Development Potential

* **Intelligent recommendations** (who to contact now, with what message).
* **Native integrations** with POS/ERP for more robust tracking.
* **Learning model by niche** (pharmacy, aesthetics, pet, etc.).
* **Behavioral events** for contextual triggers ("CS pixel").
